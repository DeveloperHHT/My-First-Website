Bryggen Homes

Tyskebryggen (German Pier), also known as Bryggen (Norwegian, Wharf), is a series of commercial Hanseatic buildings located at the entrance to the city of Bergen in Norway. Bryggen has been on UNESCO's World Cultural Heritage list since 1979. The city of Bergen was founded around 1070 with the name Tyskebryggen. About 1350 Kontors belonging to the Hanseatic League were established in the city, thus tyskebryggen became the center of the commercial activities of the Hanseatic League. Bryggen houses today serve as museums, shops, restaurants and bars. Sixty-two buildings are located from this old urban landscape.Today, Bergen is the only overseas trade center remaining within the original structures, city boundaries and landscape of the Hanseatic League. The Hanseatic League took Bergen under its control in 1350, and the German colonists who came after it directly affected Bryggen's appearance with their buildings built along narrow streets running parallel to each other.
